The Market Opportunity

A National Technician Shortage Meets the Most RV-Dense Coastline in America

There are only approximately 17,000 RV service technicians nationwide, and the industry estimates it needs at least 20% more just to meet current demand. Dealership wait times in many markets have reached six weeks — driving explosive demand for mobile mechanics who can work campground-to-campground.

The Southeast Atlantic coast is home to some of the largest RV resorts on Earth. The single mile-long stretch of South Kings Highway in Myrtle Beach alone holds three mega-parks totaling over 3,000 overnight sites. That's a mechanic's dream route.

Myrtle Beach
Grand Strand

South Carolina
7,000+ RV Sites

The highest single-corridor RV density in the Southeast. The Grand Strand stretches 60 miles of beach and welcomes over 17 million visitors annually. As of April 2026, Myrtle Beach was ranked the #1 most-searched domestic summer travel destination in the U.S. Three mega-parks sit within a 3-mile stretch of highway — an efficient, mobile-mechanic dream route.

  • Ocean Lakes — 3,447 total sites (largest campground on the East Coast)
  • Lakewood Camping Resort — 1,300 full hookup sites, oceanfront
  • Myrtle Beach Travel Park — 1,188 sites, 125-acre oceanfront
  • Sun Outdoors Myrtle Beach — 700+ RV sites near Conway
  • Myrtle Beach State Park — 278 sites, full hookup beachfront

The OBX barrier island geography is a competitive moat — the limited road access (one highway in and out) dramatically reduces competition from mainland mechanics. Camp Hatteras is the anchor with 422 full-hookup concrete-pad sites spanning 50 oceanfront-to-soundfront acres. NPS campgrounds add 200+ dry-camp sites whose visitors still need mobile mechanics.

  • Camp Hatteras RV Resort — 422 sites, ocean-to-soundfront, Rodanthe NC
  • Cape Point NPS Campground — 202 sites, Buxton (no hookups — repair traffic!)

Business Strategy

How to Hit the Ground Running

Five proven moves that turn relocation into immediate revenue — from campground manager relationships to platform partnerships.

🤝

Build Campground Relationships First

Introduce yourself to managers at Ocean Lakes, Lakewood, and Myrtle Beach Travel Park. Most parks maintain informal referral lists of trusted mobile mechanics. Getting on those three lists in a single day creates a nearly self-sustaining workflow.

🏅

Get RVIA/RVTI Certified

The RV Technical Institute offers certifications that justify higher billing rates and increase trust with campground managers. Certified techs command premium labor rates ($100+/hr at Hilton Head). It also signals professionalism to Class A motorcoach owners.

📍

The Myrtle Beach 3-Mile Route

Ocean Lakes, Lakewood, and Myrtle Beach Travel Park all sit within a 3-mile stretch of S. Kings Highway. A mobile mechanic can efficiently service customers at all three parks in a single day — 3,000+ potential customers without leaving one road.

📱

List on RVFix & Mobile Platforms

Platforms like RVFix (by Bish's RV) send repair job requests directly to local mobile techs at no upfront cost. Combined with a Google Business Profile targeting campground keywords, this provides steady inbound leads from day one without advertising spend.

🔄

The Dual-Base Year-Round Strategy

Summer in Myrtle Beach (peak tourist season). Winter in Brunswick/Golden Isles, GA (snowbird season, never closes). This two-base rotation captures both peak seasons with minimal dead time — solving the exact St. James off-season problem permanently.

⚡

OBX Geographic Moat

The Outer Banks' single-road access means mainland mechanics rarely make the trip. A local mechanic becomes essentially the only option for 422+ Camp Hatteras guests and 200+ NPS dry campers needing repairs. Low competition, captive market.
